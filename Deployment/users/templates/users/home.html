Proper cooling systems and regular checks are essential for managing heat dissipation and ensuring equipment longevity.

Power Failurer

Power failure can lead to sudden stoppages in machine operation, causing unexpected downtime and potential damage to machinery. Causes may include electrical faults, inadequate power supply, or equipment malfunctions. Implementing backup power systems and regular maintenance can help mitigate these issues.







Random Failure

Random failures are unpredictable and can occur without warning due to unforeseen issues or defects. These types of failures can be challenging to diagnose and address. Implementing comprehensive monitoring systems and performing routine inspections can help in early detection and mitigation.







Tool Wear Failure

Tool wear failure occurs when tools used in machinery degrade over time due to friction and use. This can lead to decreased performance and accuracy. Regular inspection and replacement of worn tools are crucial for maintaining operational efficiency and product quality.
